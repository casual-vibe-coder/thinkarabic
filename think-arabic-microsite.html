The pathway

Every belt maps to the international CEFR standard, so your rank isn't our opinion — it's a level the rest of the world recognises. You earn the next one by speaking, live, with a native speaker. Not by finishing videos.

HOW YOU RANK UP

A live assessment, with a stranger

You sit with a native speaker you've never met and hold a real conversation. Not a written test, not a memorised script — because a script proves nothing. Mistakes are expected. Recovering from them is the skill being measured.

Room capacity

14 seats. That's the room.

Seven men in a two-hour class is already the limit of what's useful — everyone has to actually speak. So a room caps at 14 across the week. When it's full we close it and open a waiting list. When enough men are waiting, we bring in a teacher and open the next room.

What a class is

Four formats. You speak more than the teacher.

No homework. No presentations. No slides to sit through. You come, you talk, you get corrected, you leave better.

FORMAT 01

Choose a topic

The teacher puts up ten topics — life lessons, social media and children, travel, work. You pick one and talk. The room reacts, questions, disagrees. Vocabulary gets handed to you as you reach for it.

FORMAT 02

Describe the image

One evocative image on screen. Describe it, interpret it, argue about what it means. It forces the language out of you sideways — you stop translating and start reaching.

FORMAT 03

The hot seat

The room votes one brother into the seat. Everyone questions him on a theme — how Islam shaped you as a father, what you'd change about your twenties. Nowhere to hide, and that's the point.

FORMAT 04

The debate

You're assigned a side — homeschool or public school — whether you believe it or not. Two minutes to argue it in Arabic. The room votes the stronger case. Competitive, and men try harder when they might lose.

Straight answers

Before you ask

Am I good enough to be in the room?

If you can hold a basic conversation — introduce yourself, answer a question, talk about your family and your day without a script — you're Blue Belt and you're ready. If you're not there yet, don't force it: take the free Learn to Read course and join the White → Blue waiting list. You'll get more from the room when you can use it.

What if I freeze or make a fool of myself?

Every man in that room has frozen. The teacher regulates the difficulty per person — a fresh Blue Belt gets a question he can answer, a Brown Belt gets one that stretches him. Mistakes aren't the risk; silence is. Sit in on a trial class and watch before you decide.

Is two hours a week actually enough?

It's enough to keep the language alive and moving, which is more than most men get. It isn't a substitute for a structured course — if you want a promised outcome with a plan and a guarantee, that's the cohort, not the open mat.

Why is there a waiting list?

Because the room is capped at 14 and we won't ruin the class to take your money. If it's full, you wait for a seat or for the next room to open. We open a new room when enough brothers are waiting to justify a teacher for it.

Questions

The honest answers

Why is so much of it free?

Because information isn't what's scarce. You can find grammar explanations anywhere, including from us, for nothing. What you can't get for free is a teacher in a capped room pulling the language out of you twice a week. We give away the first and charge for the second.

What's the difference between $35 and $50?

One night a week versus two. That's it — no hidden tier, no secret content. If you can only make Tuesdays, take Premium. If you want the reps, take VIP; it's the same room, twice.

Can I try before I pay?

Yes. Start a free trial and sit in on real classes. And before even that, you can join free and watch a full two-hour recording end to end. We'd rather you saw the thing than trusted a promise.

Can I cancel?

Any time, yourself. But be aware the room is capped at 14 — if you leave and there's a waiting list, your seat goes to the next brother, and you'd rejoin at the back of it. That's not a threat, it's just how a full room works.

Is there a contract or a minimum term?

No. Month to month. The class has to be worth paying for every single month, which is the way it should be.

Still deciding?

Then don't decide yet. Join free, watch a class, read the room. Nobody will call you.
